# TACO — ACM Transactions on Architecture and Code Optimization

**Website:** [taco.acm.org](https://taco.acm.org)
**Tier:** Top | **Tier-1:** Yes

## Overview

ACM journal on architecture and code optimization. Despite being a journal, TACO-accepted papers are presented at MICRO (the IEEE/ACM International Symposium on Microarchitecture), making it functionally similar to a conference venue. Dr. Song specifically noted this should be tracked as a conference-equivalent.

## Submission Details

- **Rolling review:** Submit any time; no fixed deadline.
- Papers are reviewed within approximately 3 months of submission.
- To present at MICRO, your paper must be accepted before MICRO's camera-ready deadline (~July). Submit at least 4 months before MICRO to be safe.
- MICRO is typically held in October–November.
- The deadline shown in the timeline (~July 1) is a soft target for MICRO presentation, not an official deadline.

## Review Process

- Journal-style review: 3–4 reviewers, major/minor revision cycles possible.
- Revisions are common; plan for at least one revision round.

## Misc Notes

- Because review is rolling, you can submit a polished paper at any time rather than waiting for a deadline cycle.
- MICRO acceptance gives a high-visibility presentation slot. Accepted TACO papers can also be presented at other affiliated venues.
